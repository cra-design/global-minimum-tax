Filing a return

The first year of application of the Global Minimum Tax Act (GMTA) applies to qualifying multinational enterprise (MNE) groups for fiscal years that begin on or after December 31, 2023.

Information on how to file a GloBE Information Return (GIR), a Global Minimum Tax Return and a GIR Notification will be available in Fall 2025.

On this page

  • Who must file
  • Who can file on your behalf
  • Before you file
  • How to file
  • When to file

Who must file

A member of a qualifying MNE group must file for a particular fiscal year when the group has a constituent entity located in Canada for the fiscal year.

What is a qualifying MNE group

A qualifying MNE group is an MNE group with €750 million or more in revenue reported in the consolidated financial statements of its ultimate parent entity (UPE) (in at least 2 of the 4 fiscal years immediately preceding the particular fiscal year), and is not composed exclusively of excluded entities for the particular fiscal year.

The member must file one or more of the following returns:

  • GloBE Information Return (GIR)
  • Global Minimum Tax Return
  • GIR Notification

When filing a GIR

Answer interactive questions

To determine which entities must file a GIR with the CRA, use the following interactive questions.

A text version is available if needed.

If the interactive questions do not appear, view the text version.

Choose the options that apply to your MNE group and scroll down for more questions and results.

Does the MNE group have a designated filing entity located in Canada?

  • Yes, a designated filing entity is located in Canada.
  • No, there is no designated filing entity located in Canada.

    Is the MNE group's ultimate parent entity located in Canada?

    • Yes, the ultimate parent entity is located in Canada.
    • No, the ultimate parent entity is not located in Canada.

      Does the MNE group have a qualifying foreign filing entity?

      • Yes, there is a qualifying foreign filing entity.

        Did the CRA notify the MNE group that it has not received a GIR from the qualifying foreign filing entity’s jurisdiction?

        • Yes, the CRA notified the MNE group that it has not received a GIR from the qualifying foreign filing entity’s jurisdiction.
        • No, the CRA has not notified the MNE group that it has not received a GIR from the qualifying foreign filing entity’s jurisdiction.
      • No, there is no qualifying foreign filing entity.

        Does the MNE group have an ultimate parent entity or designated filing entity outside Canada that filed the GIR directly with the CRA on or before the GIR due date?

        • Yes, an ultimate parent entity or designated filing entity outside Canada has filed the GIR directly with the CRA on or before the GIR due date.
        • No, neither an ultimate parent entity nor a designated filing entity outside Canada has filed the GIR directly with the CRA on or before the GIR due date.

Use this version if you do not want to answer interactive questions.

One of the following constituent entities of a qualifying MNE group must file a GloBE information return with the CRA:

  • The designated filing entity must file when there is one that is located in Canada
  • The ultimate parent entity must file when the following conditions are all met:
    • The ultimate parent entity is located in Canada
    • The MNE group does not have a designated filing entity located in Canada
  • Each constituent entity located in Canada or a single appointed constituent entity must file when the following conditions are all met:
    • The MNE group does not have an ultimate parent entity or designated filing entity located in Canada
    • The MNE group does not have an ultimate parent entity or designated filing entity outside Canada that filed the GIR directly with the CRA on or before the GIR due date
    • Either the MNE group does not have a qualifying foreign filing entity or it has a qualifying foreign filing entity, but the CRA notified the MNE group that it has not received a GIR from the qualifying foreign filing entity's jurisdiction

When filing a Global Minimum Tax Return

Each person who is liable to pay tax under Part 2 or Part 3 of the Act, or each Canadian filing entity, must file a Global Minimum Tax Return with the CRA.

When filing a GIR Notification

If a qualifying foreign filing entity is filing the GIR for the MNE group with the tax authority of the jurisdiction where it is located, each constituent entity located in Canada, or a designated notification entity must file a GIRN with the CRA.

Who can file on your behalf

A constituent entity or person may be appointed to file on behalf of all constituent entities or persons that must file with the CRA.

When filing a GIR

If you are filing a GIR, you may appoint a designated filing entity or a designated local entity.

When filing a Global Minimum Tax Return

If you are filing a Global Minimum Tax Return and more than one person in respect of your MNE group is required to file a GMTR for a fiscal year, one of those persons who is resident in Canada may be appointed as a Canadian filing entity.

When filing a GIR Notification

If you are filing a GIR Notification and more than one constituent entity of a qualifying MNE group is required to notify the CRA for a fiscal year of a qualifying foreign filing entity, one of those constituent entities that are located in Canada may be appointed as a designated notification entity.

Before you file

When filing a GIR

You must have the required information to complete the data points in the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) GloBE Information Return.

When filing a Global Minimum Tax Return

You will need to provide your account identifiers, contact information, as well as Part 2 and/or Part 3 information for the particular fiscal year.

Account identifiers
  • Name of the constituent entity or Canadian filing entity
  • Canadian business number
  • Name of the MNE group
  • Name of the UPE
  • Country code
  • Tax identification number of the UPE
Contact information
  • Name of the primary contact
  • Email of the primary contact
  • Phone number of the primary contact
Part 2 information
  • Name of the relevant UPE located in Canada
  • Business number of the relevant UPE
  • Name of the low-tax entity
  • Jurisdiction where the low-tax entity is located
  • Tax identification number of the low-tax constituent entity used in the GIR
  • Amount of Part 2 tax payable by the UPE in Canada in the currency of the GIR
  • Amount of Part 2 tax payable by the UPE in Canada in Canadian dollars
Part 3 information
  • Name of the constituent entity and its business number (BN15?)
  • GloBE income of the constituent entity in Canadian dollars
  • Domestic top-up amount of the constituent entity
  • Total amount of domestic minimum top-up tax payable (DMTT) in Canada as reported in GIR
  • Total amount of DMTT payable in Canada as reported in the GIR converted in Canadian dollars
  • Total GloBE income of all constituent entities located in Canada as reported in the GIR
  • Total GloBE income of all constituent entities located in Canada as reported in the GIR converted in Canadian dollars

When filing a GIR Notification

You will need to provide your account identifiers, contact information, and other information to satisfy the notification requirement under the GMTA for the particular fiscal year.

Account identifiers
  • Name of constituent entity or designated notification entity
  • Canadian business number
  • Name of the MNE group
  • Name of the UPE
  • Jurisdiction of the UPE
  • Tax identification number TIN of the UPE
  • Business number of the UPE in Canada (if any)
  • If a designated notification entity is filing on behalf of other constituent entities:
    • Name of each constituent entity and their Canadian business number
Contact information
  • Name of the primary contact
  • Email of the primary contact
  • Phone number of the primary contact
Other information to provide
  • Legal name of the qualifying foreign filing entity
  • Jurisdiction in which the qualifying foreign filing entity is located
  • TIN of the qualifying foreign entity in the filing jurisdiction
  • Name of the constituent entity and its Canadian business number if the GIR notification was filed by a different entity last year (optional)

When to file

The deadline by which the forms must be filed for affected fiscal filing periods ending December 31, 2024, or prior is June 30, 2026.

However, depending on a qualifying MNE group’s fiscal year, one of the following later dates is the due date for a qualifying MNE group’s forms:

  • 18 months after the last day of the fiscal year, if it is the first fiscal year that an entity of the qualifying MNE group is subject to:
    • Part 3 of the Act
    • a qualified IIR
    • a qualified UTPR [NEW]
      • A qualified Undertaxed Profit Rule (UTPR), for a fiscal year, means a UTPR that has qualified status (including transitional qualified status) for the fiscal year as determined by the Inclusive Framework and published on the website of the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development.[NEW POP-UP]
  • 15 months after the last day of the fiscal year, in any other case

Designated filing entity

A designated filing entity refers to a constituent entity within an MNE group, excluding the UPE, that has been appointed by the group to submit the GIR on its behalf. This entity must be located in a jurisdiction that has both of the following:

  • a Qualifying Competent Authority Agreement in place with the implementing jurisdictions where the GIR will be exchanged
  • a Qualified IIR or a Qualified UTPR

For information about Competent Authority Agreements, refer to: Competent Authority Agreements and Notices.

Designated local entity

A designated local entity refers to a constituent entity within an MNE group located in Canada and designated by other Canadian constituent entities of the group to file the GIR. However, the designation of a local entity does not guarantee that the GIR will be exchanged with other implementing jurisdictions.

Qualified Income Inclusion Rule

A qualified Income Inclusion Rule (IIR) for a fiscal year means an IIR that has qualified status (including transitional qualified status) for the fiscal year as determined by the Inclusive Framework and published on the website of the OECD.

Qualified Undertaxed Profit Rule

A qualified Undertaxed Profit Rule (UTPR), for a fiscal year, means a UTPR that has qualified status (including transitional qualified status) for the fiscal year as determined by the Inclusive Framework and published on the website of the OECD.

Qualifying foreign filing entity

A qualifying foreign filing entity (QFFE) is an ultimate parent entity or a designated filing entity of the MNE group that is located in a foreign jurisdiction and that is obligated to file a GloBE information return with the tax authority of the foreign jurisdiction, provided that the foreign jurisdiction has a qualifying competent authority agreement that is in effect on or before the GloBE information return exchange date for the fiscal year. Canada must be a party to that agreement for the entity to be a QFFE.
